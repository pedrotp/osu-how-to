Prior to sending any requests to the Dropbox API, you have to tell Dropbox who you are (App Authentication) and which user is granting you access to their Dropbox account (User Authentication). To do that, you are going to use an open specification called OAuth. You can learn more about how Dropbox uses OAuth in their OAuth Guide.


App Authentication

To authenticate your app with the Dropbox API, you need to register your app to get a key, which you will use to authenticate all HTTP requests to Dropbox. The following lines from the "Setting up your app" section of the Dropbox OAuth guide explain what to do to register:

Before you can get started, you'll need to register your app with Dropbox by creating a new app in My apps. That page will guide you through the process of registering your app, including choosing which permission your app needs, and specifying an app name.

Go throught the process above to register a new app with "App folder" permission. This means it will only have access to its own app folder inside the user's dropbox, located at Apps/[YourApp]. Once you've gone through the process outlined above, you should have gotten an app key, which you will use in this tutorial by replacing [YOUR-APP-KEY] in all the code snippets going forward.

User Authentication

Ok, now comes the tricky part. To get access to your app folder inside the user's Dropbox, you need to have that user authenticate with Dropbox and give your app access. To do that, they will be directed to a Dropbox site using a special URL, where they will enter their credentials. After authorizing your app, they will be redirected back to your site where you will be able to make requests to the Dropbox API to access the files in their Apps/[YourApp] folder.
